
# Soda Sensory Perception Study

Designed and analyzed a sensory perception study examining how soda pour method and consumption style affect taste preference. This project highlights skills in experimental design, hypothesis development, data collection, statistical analysis, and interpretation of consumer preference data.

## Study Focus
The project investigated whether external factors such as serving method and straw use can influence how soda tastes to consumers. Specifically, it explored how pouring soda into a glass versus drinking it from the original container, and consuming it with or without a straw, may shape the overall sensory experience.

## Research Question
Can a combination of pouring and consumption methods change the way soda tastes?

## Hypotheses
- Soda consumed through a straw would be preferred over soda consumed without a straw.
- Soda consumed directly from the bottle or can would be preferred over soda poured into a glass.
- The combination of drinking soda through a straw from the bottle or can would receive the highest preference ratings.

## Why This Project Matters
This study connects consumer behavior, sensory perception, and statistical analysis to better understand how small external factors can shape everyday product experiences. The findings have broader relevance for product packaging, marketing, and consumer satisfaction research.
